  • Folk High School

    • As I didn't travel in my gap year after high school, I chose to spend a month of my first summer vacation off of the university to travel. I chose to travel with the Danish folk high school HöjskolenDK, because they offered a summer folk high school with interesting and useful subjects. The Danish folk high school offer non-formal adult education. We were mainly taught in the two subjects project management and teambuilding, and we was among other things taught how to develop a fruitful cooperation and how to manage a project from idea to execution.

    Furthermore we used a lot of time to do volunteer work and learning about culture understanding. The volunteer work was at the ecological farm Ridge2Reef Farm at St. Croix, where we helped with everything from planting seeds to cooking, practically everything they were doing at the farm. We learned a lot about the culture by looking at different aspects of their culture and comparing with our own. The calm and quiet island culture, where everything is alright or gonna be alright and things take the time they take was compared to our own more or less hectic everyday life at home which again was compared to the hustle and bustle of New York City where everyone is busier than the other.

    • HöjskolenDK
      • 5. June 2016 to 5. August 2016
      • Folk High School
      • Subjects:
        • Projektmanagement and teambuilding: 35 hours
        • Volunteer work and culture understanding: 40 hours

  • Continuation School

    • I chose to take a year on a continuation school in my 9th grade. A Danish continuation school is like a boarding school for pupils aged 14-18 to finish their GSCEs. I chose Lomborg Gymnastik- og Idrætsefterskole (Lomborg Gymnastics- and sports continuation school) (LGI) because of their level segregation in all subjects. That way you were in class with someone on your own level and you could optimize the learning outcome. I had A-level in all subjects. Furthermore, I chose LGI because of their wide range of sports and sparetime activities. I played a lot of handball, did a lot gymnastics and swam a lot in my time at the continuation school, where I also tried basketball and badminton.

    • Lomborg Gymnastik- og Idrætsefterskole
      • August 2010 to June 2011
      • Class of 10/11
      • 9th grade
      • Continuation School (end of Public School (GCSE))
